If you run a service-based business, scheduling is either running smoothly or slowly driving you insane. The back-and-forth emails. The no-shows. The double-bookings. The clients who text you at 10 PM asking "are you free Tuesday?"

A good scheduling tool eliminates all of that. Clients book themselves, get reminders, and show up. You get your evenings back.

But which tool? There are dozens of options, and they all claim to be the best. Let's cut through it and look at the ones that actually matter for service businesses: Calendly, Cal.com, Acuity Scheduling, and Square Appointments.

## What Every Scheduling Tool Should Do

Before we compare, here's the baseline. Any scheduling tool worth using should:

- Let clients self-book from your website or a shared link
- Sync with your calendar (Google Calendar, Outlook, etc.) to avoid double-booking
- Send automatic confirmation and reminder emails
- Let you set your available hours
- Handle time zones automatically
- Work on mobile

All four tools we're comparing do these basics well. The differences are in the details.

## Calendly: The Most Popular Option

Calendly is the name most people know. It's clean, professional, and easy to set up. It's become the default scheduling tool for a reason.

**Pricing:**
- Free: 1 event type, basic features
- Standard: ~$12/user/month
- Teams: ~$20/user/month
- Enterprise: custom

**What Calendly does well:**
- Extremely polished booking experience — clients find it easy to use
- Quick setup — you can be live in 15 minutes
- Good integrations (Zoom, Google Meet, Stripe, PayPal, CRMs)
- Routing forms to direct bookings to the right team member
- Group events and round-robin scheduling on paid plans
- Workflows for automated follow-ups and reminders

**Where Calendly falls short:**
- Free plan is very limited (one event type is restrictive)
- Customization of the booking page is limited unless you're on higher tiers
- No built-in payment processing on the free plan
- Can feel impersonal for businesses that want a branded experience
- Not built specifically for service businesses — it's more of a general scheduling tool

**Best for:** Consultants, professionals, and businesses that primarily need meeting scheduling. If your main use case is "let people book a call or meeting with me," Calendly is polished and reliable.

## Cal.com: The Open Source Alternative

Cal.com is the open-source scheduling platform that's been gaining serious momentum. It offers most of what Calendly does, with more flexibility and a free tier that's actually useful.

**Pricing:**
- Free (self-hosted): all features, you host it
- Free (cloud): 1 user, core features
- Team: ~$15/user/month
- Organization: ~$37/user/month
- Enterprise: custom

**What Cal.com does well:**
- Generous free tier — more capable than Calendly's free plan
- Open source — you can self-host for full control
- Highly customizable booking pages
- Good integration ecosystem and growing fast
- Collective and round-robin scheduling
- Developer-friendly if you want custom integrations
- No Cal.com branding on free plans (Calendly adds theirs)

**Where Cal.com falls short:**
- Newer platform — not as battle-tested as Calendly
- Self-hosting requires technical knowledge
- Some integrations aren't as polished as Calendly's
- The interface, while good, isn't as refined as Calendly's
- Fewer pre-built workflow automations

**Best for:** Businesses that want scheduling without paying much, or businesses that want maximum control and customization. Great if you have technical resources or care about open-source software. Also solid if Calendly's branding on the free plan bothers you.

## Acuity Scheduling: Built for Service Businesses

Acuity (owned by Squarespace) was built specifically for service-based businesses — salons, trainers, therapists, photographers, consultants. It shows. While Calendly focuses on meetings, Acuity focuses on appointments.

**Pricing:**
- Emerging: ~$20/month (1 staff member)
- Growing: ~$34/month (6 staff members)
- Powerhouse: ~$61/month (36 staff members)

Note: priced per account, not per user. This matters.

**What Acuity does well:**
- Built-in payment processing (Stripe, Square, PayPal) — charge at booking time
- Intake forms — collect information from clients before appointments
- Packages and subscriptions — sell bundles of sessions
- Gift certificates built in
- Tip collection
- Multiple locations and staff scheduling
- HIPAA compliance option (critical for health/wellness businesses)
- Strongest features for service-specific needs

**Where Acuity falls short:**
- No free plan (only a 7-day trial)
- Interface is functional but not as modern as Calendly or Cal.com
- Can be complex to set up with multiple services, staff, and locations
- Owned by Squarespace, which means some integration priorities favor their ecosystem
- The booking page design is customizable but can look dated

**Best for:** Service businesses that book appointments (not just meetings). If you need to collect payment at booking, offer packages, or manage multiple staff members' schedules, Acuity is purpose-built for you. Salons, fitness studios, photographers, therapists, tutors — this is your tool.

## Square Appointments: The All-in-One Option

Square Appointments is part of the Square ecosystem. If you already use Square for payment processing, this is a natural add-on. It's specifically designed for appointment-based businesses.

**Pricing:**
- Free: 1 staff member, basic features
- Plus: ~$29/month/location
- Premium: ~$69/month/location

**What Square Appointments does well:**
- Free plan for solo operators is genuinely useful
- Tight integration with Square POS and payments
- Built-in client management (mini-CRM)
- No-show protection (charge cancellation fees)
- Text message reminders included
- Instagram and Google booking integration
- Simple, clean interface

**Where Square Appointments falls short:**
- Best value if you're already in the Square ecosystem — less compelling otherwise
- Fewer integrations with non-Square tools
- Limited customization of the booking page
- Not as feature-rich as Acuity for complex service businesses
- Reporting is basic unless you're using other Square products

**Best for:** Solo operators and small service businesses already using Square for payments. If you're a barber, personal trainer, or solo consultant and you want free scheduling with payment integration, this is hard to beat.

## Comparison Table: What Matters Most

**Free plan quality:**
Square (solo) = Cal.com > Calendly >> Acuity (none)

**Payment collection at booking:**
Acuity > Square > Calendly > Cal.com

**Multi-staff scheduling:**
Acuity > Calendly Teams > Cal.com > Square

**Ease of setup:**
Calendly > Square > Cal.com > Acuity

**Customization:**
Cal.com > Acuity > Calendly > Square

**Service business features (packages, intake forms, etc.):**
Acuity >>> Square > Calendly > Cal.com

## How to Pick

The decision is simpler than you think:

**Are you a solo consultant or freelancer who just needs meeting booking?**
Start with Calendly free or Cal.com free. Upgrade when you need more than one event type.

**Are you a solo service provider who already uses Square?**
Square Appointments free. Done. Don't overthink it.

**Do you run a service business with staff, appointments, and payments?**
Acuity Scheduling. It's built for exactly this. The cost is worth it.

**Do you want maximum control and customization for free?**
Cal.com, especially if you can self-host.

**Are you a team that needs routing, round-robin, and meeting scheduling?**
Calendly Teams or Cal.com Teams. Both handle this well.

## What About the No-Shows?

Regardless of which tool you pick, here's what actually reduces no-shows:

1. **Send reminders.** All four tools do this. Turn them on. Email + text if possible.
2. **Require payment or a deposit at booking.** Skin in the game changes behavior. Acuity and Square handle this best.
3. **Make cancellation easy but visible.** Put the reschedule/cancel link right in the reminder. People ghost when they can't figure out how to cancel.
4. **Follow up on no-shows.** A simple "We missed you, want to rebook?" message recovers a surprising number.

## Our Bottom Line

Don't spend more than a day on this decision. All four tools work. Pick the one that fits your business type:

- General meeting scheduling: Calendly or Cal.com
- Appointment-based service business: Acuity
- Solo provider on Square: Square Appointments

Set it up, put the booking link on your website and in your email signature, and stop playing email tag with clients.
